Your goal: Maximum realism

French lace and Swiss lace are the standards for an undetectable hairline. The material is thin enough that it disappears into skin — no one sees a base, they see hair.

What to expect:

  • Hairline blends with your skin at any angle
  • Works with tape or glue; tape is easier for first-timers
  • Replacement cycle: 4–8 weeks for most wearers

Your goal: Long-lasting wear

Thin skin, dura skin, and mono bases are built to take more use between replacements. Thin skin bonds tight to the scalp; mono holds its shape under daily wear.

What to expect:

  • Replacement cycle: 8–16 weeks depending on base
  • Handles heat styling and active days well
  • Easier maintenance routine than lace

Your goal: Low-effort maintenance

Nano skin and micro skin bases have a smooth poly backing that releases cleanly from tape and glue residue. The removal and reattachment routine is the simplest of any base type.

What to expect:

  • 10–15 minute weekly maintenance routine
  • Solvent removes adhesive without scrubbing
  • Good for beginners and experienced wearers alike

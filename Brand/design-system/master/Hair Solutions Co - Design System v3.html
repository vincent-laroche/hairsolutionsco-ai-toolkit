Master design system Version 3.0 · May 2026

A quiet, considered system for people regaining their hair — and themselves.

Hair Solutions Co. designs high-end, non-surgical hair replacement for men and women living with alopecia or hair loss. The brand voice is calm, educational, never clinical or hype-driven. This document defines the visual foundations — colors, typography, spacing, components, iconography, and the rules that hold them together.

Personality
Modern, minimal, high-end, educational.
Audience
Adults navigating alopecia or hair replacement.
Voice
Confident, plain-spoken, never pitying.
Aesthetic
Editorial. Generous whitespace. Restraint.
01 — Colors
Warm ink, mid charcoal, cream.

A muted, neutral-warm palette. Light surfaces lean cool-paper; ink and obsidian carry a barely-perceptible warm tilt so the page never feels clinical. Clay is the only accent — used sparingly, on dark surfaces, never for body text.

Pairing rules

Use
  • Serif owns display, H1, H2 and pull-quotes.
  • Sans owns everything functional: H3 downward, buttons, inputs, navigation, badges.
  • Mix one serif heading with sans body. Never the other way around.
  • Italic Instrument Serif is permitted for emphasis inside serif headings only.

Measure & rhythm

Layout
  • Body copy measure: 60–72 characters.
  • Editorial measure: 38–50 characters (serif display).
  • Headings sit on their own line where possible — no two-line widows.
  • text-wrap: balance on H1/H2; pretty on paragraphs.
03 — Spacing & grid
A 4-pixel base, a 12-column grid, and a habit of restraint.

Hair Solutions Co. is an editorial brand. Generous outer margins, wide gutters, and aggressive use of negative space matter more than tight component packing. Sections breathe at 128px minimum on desktop.

05 — Iconography & illustration
Thin-stroke, geometric, never decorative.

Icons exist to clarify, not to perform. Drawn on a 24px grid with a 1.5px stroke and rounded caps. No filled glyphs. No emoji. No flat illustration. Imagery is photography — editorial portraits, hands at work, materials in close-up.

Construction

Geometry
  • 24 × 24 viewBox · 20px optical body
  • 1.5px stroke at default size
  • Rounded line caps and joins
  • No mixed weights inside one icon
  • Centred on a 1px pixel grid

Tone

What they should feel like
  • Functional, not friendly
  • Quiet at the side of a paragraph
  • No mascots, no faces, no cute
  • Never carry brand meaning alone
  • Pair with a label whenever possible

Forbidden

Do not
  • Filled / duotone glyphs
  • Coloured icons except in semantic badges
  • Emoji in any product surface
  • 3D, isometric, or sketch styles
  • Flat-vector illustrations of people or hair
Photography direction · the only illustration we use
PORTRAIT · 4:5unposed
Portraits — natural light, eye-level, no styling for the camera. The hair is the subject, not the pose.

Hands at work — close, focused, almost documentary. Tools, knots, lace held to the light.
MATERIAL · 4:5macro
Materials — macro studies of hair, lace, knots and bases. Always on Off-white or Obsidian, never coloured backdrops.
SPACE · 4:5studio
Spaces — the studio, empty or near-empty. Quiet rooms, hairline mirrors, no people front-and-centre.
06 — Brand consistency rules
The short list. Read once a quarter.

These are the rules that, if held, keep the brand looking like itself across web, email, social, packaging and print — regardless of who designed the page.

Always do

  • Lead with an editorial serif headline. Let it breathe — short measure, generous margin.
  • Use one accent (Cream or Clay) per view, on dark surfaces only.
  • Pair every photograph with a quiet caption — Geist Mono, 12px, Ink 3.
  • Keep section spacing at 128px or more on desktop. Halve on mobile.
  • Use hairline rules (1px Stone) to separate, never heavy borders.
  • Write copy that explains. Audience first; product second. Educational tone over salesy tone.
  • Speak about hair loss directly and plainly. Use the words our customers use.
  • Show real customers, real stylists, real materials. Always credit when permitted.
  • Treat whitespace as content. Leave the page room to listen.

Never do

  • No gradients. No glows. No shine. No filters that warm or tint skin.
  • No emoji on product surfaces — packaging, web, email body, paid social creative.
  • No exclamation marks in editorial copy. No URGENT! sale language.
  • No before/after stagings that imply shame. Show progression, not pity.
  • No stock illustrations of people, hair strands, or follicles.
  • No mixing Cream and Clay in the same view.
  • No Instrument Serif at small UI sizes (under 24px). It is a display face.
  • No rounded buttons over 8px radius. Pills are for badges only.
  • No coloured backgrounds beyond Off-white, Paper, Obsidian and Obsidian soft.
  • No claims of medical effect, regrowth, or cure. We make hair, not medicine.

02 Letter Spacing — By Size & Context Knockout-49 shown · same rules apply to 30 and 50
Confidence
letter-spacing: −0.015em Hero headlines 80–120px · feels muscular, dense, authoritative
At very large sizes Knockout's natural openness needs tightening. −0.015em collapses the space between glyphs into a single powerful mass.
Confidence
letter-spacing: −0.01em Mid display 48–80px · slightly tighter than default optical
Section titles and product banners in the 48–80px range. Tightened slightly for display context, still very readable.
Confidence
letter-spacing: 0em Small display 20–40px · use Knockout's natural spacing
At sizes below 40px use 0em. Knockout's natural optical spacing is calibrated for this range — don't interfere with it.

12 Voice & Tone Copy principles · taglines · do / don't examples
Brand Voice — One Sentence
Direct. Adult. Discreet.
No Pity, No Pitch.
PRINCIPLE 01
Name it plainly
Hair loss is the topic. Use the word. Euphemisms ("thinning concerns," "hairline journey") sound evasive — and our customer has already heard them all.
"Hair loss is a thing. We have a fix. Here's how it works."
PRINCIPLE 02
Confidence, not hype
Never "transform your life" or "unleash the new you." We're a tool, not a religion. Specific outcomes — gym, shower, second date — beat abstract promises every time.
"Survives the gym. Survives the shower. Survives a stranger's second look."
PRINCIPLE 03
Adult, not clinical
We're not a hospital. Knockout headlines and short verbs do the work; medical jargon and disclaimer-speak don't belong on the surface. Save the spec for the spec sheet.
"Eight months in. Nobody's noticed. That's the point."
We say
  • "Confidence starts at the top."Hero — short, declarative, owns the position.
  • "Lace front. Skin base. Mono top. Pick your weather."Product nav — playful but specific.
  • "Eight years, one mission: undetectable."About — concrete tenure, plain mission.
  • "Book a fitting. Walk out with hair."CTA — promises a verb, not a vibe.
We don't say
  • "Reveal the new you."There is no new you. There's the version of you that already exists.
  • "Begin your hair journey today."Journey is wellness-blog filler. Avoid.
  • "Combat the silent epidemic of male pattern thinning."Fear-driven, dramatic, dated.
  • "Unlock unlimited possibilities."We sell one thing. Limits are fine.

Construction rules
  • 24px grid · 2px stroke · square or round line caps only
  • Sit inside a notional 36×36 square within the 56px cell — keep 10px optical margin
  • Single color: #000 on light, #fff on dark. Never tinted.
  • Filled shapes are allowed for contrast (e.g. Skin Base) but used sparingly
Usage rules
  • Always paired with a label — never icon-only navigation
  • 32–56px in product UI · 12–16px on packaging
  • Don't introduce new icons casually. Adding one needs a brand-team review.
  • Never mix with emoji or third-party icon sets in the same context
14 Photography Direction B&W · grain · directional light · real clients · no styling props
Photography is where the brand earns trust. Default to black & white with directional light and visible grain — the discretion the customer wants reflected in the imagery itself. Color appears only in product detail shots (lace base on a light surface) and atelier process shots. Tiles below are placeholders; actual imagery is sourced from real clients with full release.

Do
  • Black & white for portraits and lifestyle. Color only for product detail and process.
  • Directional, single-source light. Visible shadow. Visible grain.
  • Real clients with signed release — never models for testimonial imagery.
  • Eye contact in portraits. Subjects look like themselves, not "after" cases.
  • Tight crops on the head — eyebrows, jaw, hairline. Trust lives in detail.
Don't
  • No before-shame photography. Before is matter-of-fact, never humiliating.
  • No artificial soft light, beauty-box rings, or skin retouching beyond cleanup.
  • No stock photography of "men in suits laughing" — disqualifying instantly.
  • Never put product on a mannequin head in marketing imagery. Worn or nothing.
  • No pastel or warm-toned color grading. Discretion reads cool, not Instagrammed.
